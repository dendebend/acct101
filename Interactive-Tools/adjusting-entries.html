Adjusting Entries Drill

Master the 6 types of adjusting entries!

The 6 Types of Adjusting Entries:

  • Prepaid → Expense: Convert prepaid assets to expenses as used (Insurance, Rent)
  • Unearned → Revenue: Convert unearned revenue liability to revenue as earned
  • Accrued Revenue: Record revenue earned but not yet received
  • Accrued Expense: Record expense incurred but not yet paid
  • Depreciation: Allocate asset cost over useful life
  • Supplies: Record supplies used as expense

Type 1: Prepaid → Expense Prepaid

1. On September 1, the company paid $12,000 for a 12-month insurance policy. It is now September 30 (end of the month).
What is the adjusting entry on September 30?
One month has passed out of 12 months. Calculate: $12,000 ÷ 12 = $1,000 per month. The adjustment converts the used portion from asset to expense.
2. On July 1, the company paid $6,000 for 6 months of rent in advance. It is now July 31 (end of the month).
What is the adjusting entry on July 31?
$6,000 for 6 months = $1,000 per month. One month of rent has been "used up" and should be expensed.
3. On October 1, the company purchased $3,600 of office supplies. By October 31, a count shows $2,800 of supplies remaining.
Note: This company initially records supplies as Prepaid Supplies (an asset). What is the adjusting entry on October 31?
Started with $3,600, now have $2,800 left. What was used? $3,600 - $2,800 = $800 used. Convert the USED amount to expense.
4. The company has a Prepaid Insurance balance of $2,400 on March 1. The policy provides coverage for 12 months. It is now March 31.
What is the adjusting entry on March 31?
$2,400 ÷ 12 months = $200 per month. Record one month's worth of insurance as expense.

Type 2: Unearned → Revenue Unearned

5. On November 1, the company received $9,000 cash for services to be performed over the next 3 months. It is now November 30.
What is the adjusting entry on November 30?
$9,000 for 3 months = $3,000 per month earned. One month has passed, so $3,000 of the liability has been earned. Reduce liability, recognize revenue.
6. The company has an Unearned Revenue balance of $12,000. During the month, the company performed services worth $4,000 that were previously unearned.
What is the adjusting entry?
Convert what was earned ($4,000) from liability to revenue. Debit the liability to reduce it, credit revenue to increase it.
7. On June 1, the company received $24,000 for a 12-month service contract. It is now June 30.
What is the adjusting entry on June 30?
$24,000 ÷ 12 months = $2,000 per month. One month of service has been provided.
8. A customer paid $5,000 in advance on August 1 for work to be completed over 5 months. It is now August 31.
What is the adjusting entry on August 31?
$5,000 ÷ 5 months = $1,000 per month. One month of work has been completed.

Type 3: Accrued Revenue Accrued Revenue

9. The company provided $2,500 of services to a customer but has not yet billed the customer or received payment.
What is the adjusting entry?
Services were performed (revenue earned) but not yet billed or collected. This creates a receivable.
10. The company has a note receivable with a principal of $10,000. Interest of $150 has been earned this month but will be collected next month.
What is the adjusting entry?
Interest has been EARNED but not yet COLLECTED. This is accrued revenue - create a receivable.
11. The company rents out part of its building. Rent of $800 for this month is owed by the tenant but has not been received yet.
What is the adjusting entry?
Rent has been EARNED (tenant used the space) but not yet RECEIVED. Record the revenue and create a receivable.
12. The company completed $3,200 of consulting work during December that will be billed to the client in January.
What is the adjusting entry on December 31?
Work completed in December = revenue earned in December, even though billing happens later.

Type 4: Accrued Expense Accrued Expense

13. Employees earned $4,500 in salaries during the last week of the month, but will not be paid until next month.
What is the adjusting entry?
Work was done (expense incurred) but payment hasn't been made yet. This is an accrued expense - create a payable.
14. The company has a note payable with interest of $250 that has accrued this month but will be paid next month.
What is the adjusting entry?
Interest has been INCURRED but not yet PAID. Record the expense and create a payable.
15. The company used $900 of utilities during December. The bill will arrive and be paid in January.
What is the adjusting entry on December 31?
Utilities were USED in December (expense incurred) but not yet billed or paid.
16. The company owes $1,200 in property taxes for this year that will be paid next year.
What is the adjusting entry?
Tax expense for THIS year is owed but not yet paid. Incurred but not paid = accrued expense.

Type 5: Depreciation Depreciation

17. The company owns equipment that costs $60,000 with an estimated useful life of 5 years and no salvage value. Using straight-line depreciation, record one year's depreciation.
What is the adjusting entry?
Straight-line: $60,000 ÷ 5 years = $12,000 per year. Credit Accumulated Depreciation (contra-asset), NOT Equipment directly.
18. A building costs $240,000 with a useful life of 20 years and no salvage value. Record one month's depreciation using straight-line.
What is the adjusting entry?
Annual depreciation: $240,000 ÷ 20 = $12,000. Monthly: $12,000 ÷ 12 = $1,000.
19. Vehicles costing $80,000 are being depreciated over 8 years with no salvage value. Record one year's depreciation.
What is the adjusting entry?
$80,000 ÷ 8 years = $10,000 per year. Use Accumulated Depreciation as the contra-asset.
20. Equipment with a cost of $36,000 has a 6-year life and $6,000 salvage value. Record one year's straight-line depreciation.
What is the adjusting entry?
Depreciable amount = Cost - Salvage = $36,000 - $6,000 = $30,000. Annual: $30,000 ÷ 6 = $5,000.

Type 6: Supplies Supplies

21. The company had $2,000 of supplies at the beginning of the month and purchased $800 more during the month. At month-end, a count shows $1,200 of supplies remaining.
What is the adjusting entry?
Beginning $2,000 + Purchased $800 = $2,800 available. Ending $1,200 remains. Used: $2,800 - $1,200 = $1,600.
22. The Supplies account shows a balance of $3,500. A physical count reveals $900 of supplies on hand.
What is the adjusting entry?
Had $3,500, now have $900 left. What was used? $3,500 - $900 = $2,600 used.
23. Office supplies of $1,800 were purchased during the month. At month-end, $600 of supplies remain unused.
What is the adjusting entry?
Purchased $1,800, have $600 left. Used: $1,800 - $600 = $1,200.
24. The Supplies account balance is $4,200. A count shows that $1,500 worth of supplies have been used during the period.
What is the adjusting entry?
The question directly tells you $1,500 was USED. Convert that amount to expense.
