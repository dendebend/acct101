1 Unit 02A: Account Types & Accounting Equation
1. Which one of the following is NOT a revenue account?
Unearned Revenue is a LIABILITY - cash received before services are performed.
2. Which of the following is NOT an expense account?
Prepaid Expense is an ASSET - it's a payment made in advance.
3. If total liabilities decreased by $30,000 during a period and stockholders' equity increased by $35,000, what was the change in total assets?
Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Change in A = Change in L + Change in E = -30,000 + 35,000
4. A corporation with total stockholders' equity of $75,000 paid a $12,000 business debt. What happened to total stockholders' equity?
Paying a debt decreases Cash (asset) and Liabilities equally. Equity is unaffected.
2 Unit 02B: Debits, Credits & Normal Balances
5. Accounts are:
Accounts track all changes (increases and decreases) for each financial statement item.
6. The left side of an account is always:
DEbit = LEft side. CREdit = Right side.
7. A credit will increase ________, but decrease ________.
Credits increase LER (Liabilities, Equity, Revenue) and decrease DEA (Dividends, Expenses, Assets).
8. Using the DEALER acronym, which accounts have a DEBIT normal balance?
DEALER: DEA = Dividends, Expenses, Assets have debit normal balances.
9. Accounts with normal CREDIT balances include:
DEALER: LER = Liabilities, Equity, Revenue have credit normal balances.
3 Unit 03A: Accounting Cycle Steps 1-4, AIS
10. A list of accounts used by a business is called the:
Chart of Accounts = master list of all accounts used by the company.
11. Which is NOT true with a double-entry accounting system?
Double-entry means debits = credits, not that every transaction has two debits!
12. What is the appropriate order for a company's general ledger?
Balance Sheet first (A, L, E), then Income Statement (R, E).
13. The process of initially recording a business transaction is called:
Journalizing = making the journal entry initially.
14. The procedure of transferring journal entries to ledger accounts is called:
Posting = transferring from journal to ledger.
15. Transactions are initially recorded in the:
The accounting cycle: Analyze ‚Üí Journalize ‚Üí Post ‚Üí Trial Balance.
16. Which contains the selected steps of the accounting cycle in the CORRECT order?
1. Journalize 2. Post 3. Analyze 4. Prepare financial statements 5. Trial balance
Analyze ‚Üí Journalize ‚Üí Post ‚Üí Trial Balance ‚Üí Financial Statements.
17. Someone who wants to investigate a specific business transaction would refer to the:
Journal shows individual transactions. Ledger shows individual accounts.
18. Someone who wants to investigate a specific account would refer to the:
The ledger contains all accounts with their balances.
19. Which error will cause a trial balance to be out of balance? An entry to record a payment on account was:
Two debits mean debits ‚â† credits, so trial balance won't balance!
20. When a trial balance balances, it indicates that:
Trial balance only proves debits = credits, not that everything is correct!
4 Unit 03B: Adjusting Entries
21. When adjusting for supplies used during the period, you would ________ Supplies Expense.
Supplies (asset) decreased, Supplies Expense (expense) increased. Expenses increase with debits.
22. When recording depreciation, you would ________ Accumulated Depreciation.
Accumulated Depreciation is a contra-asset with a credit balance.
23. To record accrued salaries (earned but not yet paid), you would ________ Salaries Expense.
Accrued expense: Debit the expense, Credit the liability (Salaries Payable).
24. When services are performed for a customer who paid in advance (unearned revenue), you would ________ Service Revenue.
Unearned Revenue (liability) ‚Üí Service Revenue (revenue). Revenue increases with credits.
25. To adjust prepaid insurance that has expired, you would ________ Insurance Expense.
Prepaid ‚Üí Expense adjustments: Debit expense, Credit prepaid (asset).
5 Unit 04: Financial Statements
26. Financial statements are usually prepared in which sequence?
I.R.B.C: Income ‚Üí Retained Earnings ‚Üí Balance Sheet ‚Üí Cash Flows.
27. Which would NOT appear on the income statement?
Dividends appear on the Retained Earnings statement, not the Income Statement.
28. Which would NOT appear on the retained earnings statement?
Retained Earnings Statement: Beginning RE + Net Income - Dividends = Ending RE.
29. A financial statement that reports accounting data at a SPECIFIC DATE is the:
Balance Sheet = "as of" a specific date. Others = "for the period ended".
30. The ending retained earnings balance appears on:
Ending RE calculated on RE statement, then flows to Balance Sheet.
31. Beginning RE = $60,000. Ending RE = $70,000. Dividends = $20,000. What was net income?
Ending RE = Beginning RE + NI - Dividends. So: 70,000 = 60,000 + NI - 20,000.
32. Beginning: Assets = $120,000, Equity = $40,000. During year: NI = $90,000, Dividends = $20,000. Ending Assets = $215,000. What are ending liabilities?
Ending Equity = 40,000 + 90,000 - 20,000 = 110,000. L = A - E = 215,000 - 110,000.
6 Unit 05: Closing Process
33. Revenue accounts are classified as ________ accounts (permanent or temporary).
Revenues, Expenses, and Dividends are temporary (closed at year-end).
34. Asset accounts are classified as ________ accounts (permanent or temporary).
Assets, Liabilities, and Equity are permanent (carry forward to next period).
35. Which accounts are closed at year-end?
All temporary accounts are closed: R, E, D.
36. In the first closing entry, revenue accounts are:
To close a revenue (credit balance), you debit it to zero.
37. In the second closing entry, expense accounts are:
To close an expense (debit balance), you credit it to zero.
38. Revenue and expense accounts are closed to ________ ________.
Income Summary is the temporary holding account during closing.
39. Income Summary is closed to ________ ________.
Net income or loss ultimately goes to Retained Earnings.
40. The correct order of the 4 closing entries is:
R.E.I.D: Revenues ‚Üí Expenses ‚Üí Income Summary ‚Üí Dividends.

50. The primary purpose of the trial balance is to:
Trial balance ONLY proves debits = credits, not that everything is correct!
