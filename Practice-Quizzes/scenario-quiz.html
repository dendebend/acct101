Scenario-Based Account Quiz

Master identifying accounts in real business transactions!

0/30 (0%)

Easy - Basic Transactions

1. The company pays $2,000 cash for rent for the current month.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Since the rent is for the CURRENT month, it's an expense that has been incurred and should be recorded now.
2. The company purchases office equipment for $5,000 cash.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Equipment is a long-term asset that will be used for multiple years, not a one-time expense.
3. The company provides services to a customer and receives $3,000 cash immediately.
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
The service has been performed (completed), so revenue has been earned.
4. The company pays $1,500 cash for employee salaries for work completed this month.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Since work was completed this month and we're paying now, this is a current expense.
5. The company borrows $10,000 from the bank and signs a promissory note.
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
A promissory note is a formal written promise to pay. Since the company owes money, this is a liability.
6. The company pays $800 cash for utilities for the current month.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Current month usage = current expense.
7. The company purchases supplies for $500 cash.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
When supplies are purchased, they're recorded as an asset. They become an expense when used.
8. The company pays $600 cash for advertising in the current month.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Since the advertising benefit is received in the current month, it's a current expense.

Medium - Common Tricky Scenarios

9. The company provides services to a customer for $4,000 on account (customer will pay later).
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
"On account" means the customer owes money - the company has a receivable.
10. The company pays $3,600 cash for a 12-month insurance policy starting next month.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Paid in advance for FUTURE coverage = Prepaid (asset). It becomes an expense as coverage is used each month.
11. The company purchases inventory for $2,000 on account (will pay the supplier later).
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
"On account" when purchasing = the company owes money to the supplier (a payable).
12. Employees worked this month but will be paid next month. The company owes $2,500 in salaries.
Which account is CREDITED in the adjusting entry?
Work done but not yet paid = accrued expense = a payable (liability).
13. The company collects $1,200 cash from a customer who owed money.
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
Collecting money owed reduces the receivable. This is NOT new revenue - revenue was recorded earlier.
14. The company pays $1,800 to a supplier for inventory purchased last month on account.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Paying off what you owe reduces the payable. The inventory was already recorded last month.
15. The company used $200 of supplies that were purchased last month.
Which account is DEBITED in the adjusting entry?
Using supplies converts the asset (Supplies) into an expense (Supplies Expense).
16. The company earns $900 of interest on a note receivable but will collect the cash next month.
Which account is DEBITED in the adjusting entry?
Interest earned but not yet collected = Interest Receivable (asset). Revenue is still recognized now.
17. The company owes $300 interest on a note payable but will pay it next month.
Which account is CREDITED in the adjusting entry?
Interest incurred but not yet paid = Interest Payable (liability). Expense is still recognized now.
18. One month of the prepaid insurance policy (from question 10) has now been used.
Which account is CREDITED in the adjusting entry?
Using up prepaid coverage converts the asset (Prepaid Insurance) into expense (Insurance Expense).
19. The company pays a $5,000 dividend to shareholders.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Dividends are NOT an expense! They're a distribution of equity. Use the "Dividends" equity account.
20. The company purchases land for $50,000 by paying $10,000 cash and signing a note payable for the rest.
In addition to Land (DR) and Cash (CR), which other account is involved?
A note payable is a formal written promise to pay. This is different from an informal account payable.

Hard - The Tricky "Gotcha" Accounts

21. The company receives $6,000 cash from a customer for services to be performed over the next 6 months.
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
Cash received BEFORE service performed = liability (we owe the service). "Unearned" means not yet earned.
22. Equipment with an original cost of $20,000 has been used for one year. Annual depreciation is $2,000.
Which account is CREDITED in the adjusting entry?
We don't reduce Equipment directly! Accumulated Depreciation is a contra-asset that tracks total depreciation over time.
23. The company estimates that $500 of accounts receivable will not be collected.
Which account is CREDITED in the adjusting entry?
We use a contra-asset account (Allowance for Doubtful Accounts) instead of directly reducing AR when estimating.
24. One month has passed and the company earned $1,000 of the revenue that was previously unearned (from question 21).
Which account is DEBITED in the adjusting entry?
Converting unearned revenue to earned revenue means reducing the liability and recognizing revenue.
25. The company buys back 100 shares of its own stock for $2,000 cash.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Treasury Stock is a contra-equity account for a company's own shares that it reacquired.
26. The company issues a $30,000 long-term bond to investors.
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
Bonds are a specific type of long-term liability with their own account name.
27. The company acquires a patent (intangible asset) for $15,000 cash.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Patents are intangible assets. Like equipment, they're assets when purchased, not expenses.
28. The company pays $4,000 for rent covering the next 4 months.
Which account is DEBITED in this transaction?
Paid in advance for FUTURE rent = Prepaid Rent (asset). Compare to question 1 where rent was for the CURRENT month.
29. The company records amortization of $500 on the patent from question 27.
Which account is CREDITED in the adjusting entry?
For intangibles, we typically credit the asset directly (Patents) rather than using a contra-asset.
30. The company owner invests an additional $25,000 cash into the business in exchange for common stock.
Which account is CREDITED in this transaction?
Owner investments are NOT revenue! They increase equity through the Common Stock account.
