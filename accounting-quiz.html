6 Interest Calculations
23. A company borrowed $10,000 at 6% annual interest on October 1. How much interest expense should be accrued by December 31 (3 months)?
Interest = Principal × Rate × Time
Interest = $10,000 × 6% × (3/12)
Convert 6% to 0.06. Time is 3 months out of 12 months = 3/12 or 0.25
24. The adjusting entry to record accrued interest expense would be:
It's accrued (not yet paid). Expense increases (debit), liability increases (credit Interest Payable).
25. You loaned $20,000 to another company at 9% annual interest on July 1. How much interest revenue should be recognized by December 31 (6 months)?
Formula: $20,000 × 9% × (6/12). Note: This is revenue, not expense!
