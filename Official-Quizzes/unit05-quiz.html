05 Closing Entries
1. To close out an account means to make its balance equal to ___.
Think about what happens to temporary accounts at period end - they need to have no balance left.
2. If Income Summary has a credit balance, it is closed out via a ___.
To close an account with a credit balance, you need the opposite side to bring it to zero.
3. The balance of Retained Earnings on the adjusted trial balance is the balance at the ___ of the period.
The adjusted trial balance happens BEFORE closing entries, so RE still has its old balance.
4. The balance of Retained Earnings on the post-closing trial balance is the balance at the ___ of the period.
POST-closing means after all closing entries are done, so RE has been updated with net income/loss.
5. Accumulated Depreciation is closed out at the end of each period.
Accumulated Depreciation is a PERMANENT (contra-asset) account. Only TEMPORARY accounts get closed.
6. The post-closing trial balance contains all ___ accounts.
After closing entries, all temporary accounts have zero balance. What type of accounts remain?
7. If Income Summary has a debit balance during the closing process prior to being closed out, it indicates that a company has a net ___ for the period.
Debit balance in Income Summary means Expenses > Revenues. What does that indicate?
8. If Income Summary has a credit balance during the closing process prior to being closed out, it indicates that a company has a net ___ for the period.
Credit balance in Income Summary means Revenues > Expenses. This is profitable!
9. The purpose of closing entries is to close out all ___ accounts.
Remember R.E.I.D.? Revenues, Expenses, Income Summary, Dividends - these all get closed.
10. If the company sustained a net loss for the year, ___ ___ is debited.
Net loss DECREASES this equity account. Remember R.E.I.D. step 3?
11. If the company sustained a net income for the year, ___ ___ is debited.
To close this account with a CREDIT balance (net income), we debit it. Then credit Retained Earnings.
12. ___ has/have the effect of decreasing Retained Earnings without reducing income.
This is the "D" in R.E.I.D. - a distribution to shareholders that reduces RE but isn't an expense.
